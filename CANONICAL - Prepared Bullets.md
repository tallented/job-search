## First-Draft Usage Rules

- First drafts must copy positioning lines, summary blocks, core skills blocks, and bullets exactly.
- Do not rewrite during initial tailoring. If a bullet needs edits, create and validate a revised version here first.
- Square brackets describe audience, role fit, or theme. Curly braces describe metadata, status, source, and constraints.
- Keep duplicate-ish variants when they differ in line behavior, emphasis, or resume-track fit.

## Tag Legend

- {Length: Short | Medium | Long} indicates practical copy-fit/page-budget behavior, not strict word count.
- {Status: Ready} means safe for exact first-draft copy from a canonical resume.
- {Status: Review} means useful source material that should be validated before becoming first-draft copy.
- {Conditional: ...} marks context-specific items that should not be used generically.
- {Source: ...} identifies the originating document or resume family.
