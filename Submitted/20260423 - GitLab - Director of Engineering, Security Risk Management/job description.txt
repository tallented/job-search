Role overview:
GitLab is hiring an engineering lead to evolve its Security Risk Management stage into a platform for vulnerability analysis and remediation at enterprise scale. The role combines director-level engineering leadership with deep distributed-systems architecture, security-platform strategy, and product partnership.

Core responsibilities:
- Design distributed systems for vulnerability data across thousands of repositories, millions of commits, and complex dependency graphs.
- Drive storage architecture decisions for very large security datasets across time-series, graph, and document-oriented patterns.
- Architect scalable analysis pipelines that ingest vulnerability feeds, correlate findings across multiple security tools, and deliver actionable intelligence.
- Lead the evolution from monolithic security scanning toward microservices-based, event-driven vulnerability-management systems.
- Establish technical standards for observability, performance, and fault tolerance in security-critical systems.
- Mentor senior engineers in distributed systems, database optimization, and large-scale architecture.
- Partner with product management, UX, and design on the end-to-end user journey for AppSec professionals and developers.
- Own technical roadmap areas such as vulnerability correlation, risk scoring, and automated remediation workflows.
- Lead incident response for availability and performance issues in customer-facing security systems.

Key qualifications:
- 10+ years of software engineering experience
- 5+ years leading distributed systems at scale
- Deep experience with high-throughput, low-latency systems and complex data models
- Experience with relational, time-series, graph, and distributed key-value storage patterns
- Experience with stream processing and event-driven architecture
- Hands-on Kubernetes and cloud-native observability experience
- Security domain knowledge in vulnerability assessment, static analysis, dependency scanning, or application security testing
- Track record leading and growing engineering teams of 40+ engineers
- Strong executive communication and cross-team leadership
