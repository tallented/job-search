Role Description

Dropbox is looking for an Engineering Manager to lead its Privacy Engineering team. The role leads a team of 5-7 engineers building privacy-first infrastructure, automation, and user-facing controls that safeguard customer data and support trust and compliance.

The team works on systems such as:

- Data subject request (DSR) processing
- Data classification
- Preference management / cookie consent
- Privacy settings

The role also helps evolve Dropbox’s AI-era privacy posture by leveraging automation and machine learning to anticipate risks and prevent privacy-invasive events.

This position sits at the intersection of Engineering, Legal, Security, and Product, and partners with those stakeholders to embed privacy into the foundation of Dropbox products while proactively supporting compliance with global privacy regulations.

Responsibilities

- Grow and develop a high-performing team of engineers with strong ownership and impact expectations.
- Build a healthy, collaborative culture aligned with Dropbox values.
- Ensure the team delivers strong output and continuously seeks outsized impact.
- Set direction for the team and anticipate strategic and scaling challenges through long-term planning.
- Help craft technical and product strategy.
- Participate in or manage services with on-call expectations where applicable.

Requirements

- BS, MS, or PhD in Computer Science or related technical field, or equivalent technical experience.
- Minimum of 8 years of industry experience as a software developer.
- 3+ years of experience managing engineering teams.
- Experience implementing or leading efforts around data minimization, differential privacy, data subject rights, and secure data architecture.
