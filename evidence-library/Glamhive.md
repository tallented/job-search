# Glamhive

Validated: 2026-04-09 via resume-tailoring QA with Chris Tallent.

## Scope Summary

- Role: Technical Co-Founder & Chief Technology Officer
- Context: zero-to-one startup product build with high ambiguity
- Built the initial MVP personally before the company had funding to staff up
- Launched the first production version on AWS as the sole engineer
- Most of the early build was done for equity before the Seattle Angel Conference win
- Team growth happened after the Seattle Angel Conference VII investment provided room to hire

## MVP / Founding Build

- Personally built the first MVP end to end
- Scope included front end, back end, data model, architecture, and AWS launch
- This was not just technical oversight; Chris was the hands-on builder for the initial product
- Strong zero-to-one founder signal for roles that value product intuition plus execution under constraint

## Product / Stakeholder Partnership

- Worked with C-level and product stakeholders to balance business urgency against engineering quality and pace of work
- Helped shape technical roadmap decisions by clarifying what was easier, what was harder, and where sequencing mattered

## Delivery Model Under Ambiguity

- Treated ambiguity as normal startup operating reality
- Preferred staged, end-to-end delivery every two weeks so stakeholders could react to working software rather than abstract plans
- Used Agile delivery to avoid long waterfall cycles where feedback arrives too late

## Cross-Team / Operations

- Consistently worked across teams to maintain communication and align product, UX, QA, and engineering work
- Directly involved in incident response, escalation, diagnosis, and postmortem work as part of the CTO role
- One primary reason for using South American nearshore staffing was to preserve enough working-hour overlap for the full tech team to join shared daily and weekly calls

## Resume Takeaways

- Useful for roles that value:
  - zero-to-one product leadership
  - founder-level MVP execution
  - hands-on full-stack delivery under extreme resource constraint
  - startup ambiguity tolerance
  - founder-level technical prioritization
  - agile staged delivery in fast-moving environments
