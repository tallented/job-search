# Operating Model - CTO

Validated: 2026-04-09 via resume-tailoring QA with Chris Tallent.

## Role In Practice

- In smaller-company CTO roles, Chris typically operates as:
  - engineering leader
  - lead architect
  - security/platform decision-maker
  - infrastructure owner or lead infrastructure architect
- Common pattern: bootstrap the first version or critical parts of a system personally, then transition day-to-day implementation to teams while retaining ownership of architecture, security design, standards, and technical direction

## Architecture / Platform Ownership

- Typically sets:
  - platform architecture
  - security architecture
  - technology platform choices
  - infrastructure direction
- In smaller companies, often owns the AWS environment directly or works with a single DevOps lead who handles implementation details

## Team Design Philosophy

- Strong preference for engineers who can work full-stack, especially on smaller teams
- Typical structure includes a UI lead and a back-end lead, but the rest of the team is expected to be comfortable working across both areas
- Reasoning: avoid bottlenecks where work stops because the available developers are too specialized in one layer

## Product Partnership

- Active participant in product discussions across most CTO roles
- Typically involved from:
  - concept formation
  - feature prioritization
  - roadmap shaping
  - UX design review for technical feasibility
  - epic and sprint breakdown
  - high-level estimation of time and budget
- In internally driven products, often has a major hand in shaping how the product works and what features it should include

## Prioritization / Quality Philosophy

- Frequent responsibility is balancing business urgency against engineering quality, speed, and tech debt
- Regularly educates C-suite stakeholders that "everything at once" is not a real prioritization strategy
- Common approach:
  - explain which items are easier vs harder
  - highlight sequencing dependencies
  - push back when urgency would create disproportionate quality or debt costs

## Delivery Model Under Ambiguity

- Default philosophy is Agile / staged delivery, especially in ambiguous startup or modernization work
- Preferred pattern is to deliver a working end-to-end slice every two weeks
- Goal is to surface real feedback quickly and avoid discovering six months later that the product was not what stakeholders actually wanted
- May use pilots or experiments when needed, but those are fed back into the staged rollout cadence rather than becoming isolated side work

## Incident Response / Operations

- In most CTO roles outside large enterprises, Chris has been directly involved in:
  - incident response
  - escalation
  - diagnosis
  - postmortems
- Often de facto on-call for backend, integration, or infrastructure issues even when another engineer is nominally assigned
- Typically helps design support, escalation, and operational response processes

## Cross-Team Coordination

- Common responsibility is making sure communication is happening across engineering, QA, UX/product, operations, and external stakeholders
- Regularly helps prioritize work across those functions so delivery remains coherent and deadlines are met

## Resume Takeaways

- Useful for leadership resumes where the title was `CTO` but the target role is:
  - engineering manager
  - director / head of engineering
  - principal-plus architecture leader
- Best used to explain that the role was not "executive only"; it combined technical leadership, architecture, product partnership, and operational ownership
