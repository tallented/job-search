# Lela.com

Validated: 2026-04-09 via resume-tailoring QA with Chris Tallent.

## Scope Summary

- Role progression: stepped up from senior developer into the CTO role after a management shakeup and pivot
- Context: original founders were pushed out, prior CTO left, and confidence in technical direction and engineering delivery was low

## Turnaround / Trusted Advisor Story

- After stepping into the CTO role, Chris pivoted the team in a new architectural direction over the Christmas break
- Built a technical roadmap aligned to the revised product roadmap objectives
- Helped restore confidence among the C-suite, board of directors, and primary investor

## Board / Executive Communication

- Built visual slide decks for the board of directors
- Walked the C-level team, board, and primary investor through:
  - upcoming roadmap
  - turnaround strategy
  - screenshots and demos of work produced
- Purpose was not just status reporting; it was rebuilding confidence in both the product direction and the engineering team
- Chris specifically recalls receiving applause from the board and investor after the first major post-pivot presentation

## Product / Prioritization Leadership

- Spent substantial time educating the rest of the C-suite on how prioritization should work
- Repeatedly had to explain that "we need to do everything at once" is not a real prioritization framework
- Worked with C-suite stakeholders to balance urgency, quality, and delivery pace

## Enterprise Software Evaluation

- Participated in an RFP-style evaluation for a large enterprise software package in the Siebel / CRM category
- Exposure here was to enterprise-package assessment and selection process, not long-term procurement ownership

## Delivery Model

- Used Agile / staged delivery in a high-ambiguity environment
- Preferred to deliver working feature slices every two weeks so stakeholders could refine expectations against real software

## Experimentation / Recommendation Tuning

- Used Optimizely for A/B testing while refining recommendation and product behavior
- Also used dynamic JavaScript rewriting tooling to support experimentation and behavior changes without tightly coupling every change to backend service releases

## Cross-Team / Operations

- Consistently worked across teams to align product, UX, QA, and engineering work
- Directly involved in incident response, escalation, diagnosis, and postmortem work as part of the CTO role

## Resume Takeaways

- Useful for roles that value:
  - turnaround leadership
  - trusted technical advisor signal
  - executive and board communication
  - architectural pivot under pressure
  - CTOs who can restore confidence in a struggling engineering org
