Role summary:
- Director-level engineering leadership role for Instacart's Catalog Content team.
- Remote-friendly across the U.S. and Canada.
- Own the next chapter of the "agentic catalog" strategy, using distributed systems, data pipelines, and AI/ML automation to improve product-content quality, speed, scale, coverage, and freshness.
- Lead a multi-team engineering organization of about 40+ total reports, including managers and senior ICs.
- Partner with Product, Machine Learning, Data Science, Design, Operations, Retail partners, and Brand partners.
- Build operating mechanisms, set goals, develop talent, and align teams to measurable outcomes.
- Own reliability and quality through availability, latency, and accuracy SLAs; on-call; incident response; observability; and data-quality guardrails.
- Prioritize roadmap and experiments that improve discovery, search relevance, substitution quality, pricing accuracy, and regulatory compliance.
- Drive build/buy decisions, vendor selection, and budget stewardship for capabilities such as vector search, embeddings, OCR, image understanding, and content moderation.
- Ensure privacy, security, and brand safety.

Minimum qualifications:
- 15+ years professional software engineering experience building and operating large-scale platforms or data-intensive systems.
- 7+ years leading engineering managers and multiple teams, with direct responsibility for 30+ engineers.
- Large-scale data pipelines and services, batch and streaming, in AWS or GCP.
- Event-driven architectures and microservices.
- Platform reliability and data quality ownership with SLAs/SLOs, on-call, and incident management.
- Production ML/AI systems, including LLMs, information retrieval, content understanding, or automated data enrichment.
- Hiring, coaching, performance management, and development of engineering managers and senior engineers.
- Cross-functional roadmap and business-outcome delivery with Product, Data Science/ML, and Operations.

Preferred qualifications:
- E-commerce catalog systems, product taxonomy/ontology, data normalization, or content quality at marketplace scale.
- GenAI and agentic frameworks, orchestration, tool use, RAG, vector databases, embeddings, and multimodal models.
- Search/relevance, knowledge graphs, entity resolution, or compliance workflows such as nutrition, allergens, or age-restricted items.
- Platforms adopted by 100+ internal users or powering revenue-critical customer experiences.
- Vendor ecosystems and annual engineering budgets tied to infrastructure and third-party services.
- Experimentation and evaluation frameworks for content quality, including offline/online metrics, A/B testing, data drift, model performance, and observability.
