# Search Strategy - 20260412 - Executive Scale

Last updated: 2026-04-12

## Objective

Primary goal: land a role that increases large-company scale credibility on the path toward `CTO` / `EVP Technology` at a materially larger company.

Secondary goal: capture top-tier brand value when it also improves future scale, compensation, and marketability.

Preferred functional destination: run an `application / product engineering` organization, especially one building customer-facing software, workflow-heavy SaaS, and greenfield or major-modernization products.

Core rule: prefer roles that increase at least two of these in a meaningful way:

- engineering headcount
- management layers
- annual budget
- governance / security / compliance burden
- executive visibility
- platform complexity
- business criticality

Blunt shorthand: `Senior Director` at a serious company can be a stronger bridge than `CTO` at another small company.

## Functional Preference Order

Default search bias going forward:

1. `Application / Product Engineering` leadership
2. `Platform / DevEx / Cloud` bridge roles that stay close to product delivery
3. `Security / Identity / Corporate Engineering` only when brand, scope, compensation, or trajectory are unusually strong

Working thesis:

- Chris' strongest differentiated story is building apps, especially greenfield apps, and leading teams that turn messy business workflows into durable customer-facing software.
- Platform roles are still useful when they clearly improve scale credibility and keep the path open to larger application-engineering ownership.
- Security-heavy and corporate-engineering roles are valid opportunistic plays, but they should not dominate the search just because the market surfaces many of them.

Best company types:

- product-led SaaS and application companies with meaningful customer-facing complexity
- workflow-heavy software companies where application development is strategically important
- companies building new product lines, modernizing legacy applications, or scaling multi-team product engineering
- PE-backed companies that need modernization and operating discipline
- late-stage SaaS companies outgrowing founder-led engineering
- enterprise software companies with platform, reliability, or security pressure
- regulated or compliance-heavy businesses
- companies operationalizing AI, automation, or post-acquisition integration

What makes a role a good bridge:

- `30-100+` engineers or a believable path to that scope
- managers or directors under you
- budget ownership
- security / compliance / governance exposure
- regular cadence with CEO / CFO / Product / GTM leadership
- clear business scoreboards such as margin, reliability, cost, delivery speed, or revenue support

Avoid:

- tiny-company CTO roles with basically the same scope as prior roles
- title-inflated VP roles with very small teams
- strategy / innovation roles without delivery ownership

### Lane 2: Top-Tier Brand Management Lane

Secondary lane, but highly attractive because it adds recognizable scale and brand proof.

Target titles:

- `Engineering Manager`
- `Senior Engineering Manager`
- `Manager, Software Engineering`
- `Manager, Platform Engineering`
- `Manager, Infrastructure Engineering`
- `Director of Engineering`
- `Director, Platform Engineering`
- `Director, Infrastructure Engineering`
- `Director, SRE`
- `Director, Reliability Engineering`
- `Head of Developer Productivity`
- `Head of Engineering` at an obviously strong brand

Best company profiles:

- Netflix-level or other elite engineering brands
- product/application engineering teams at elite brands
- platform, infrastructure, reliability, developer productivity, data platform, and AI platform organizations that are close to customer-facing product delivery
- companies known for strong engineering standards, internal tooling, and operational rigor

Why this lane matters:

- strong brand signal
- proven exposure to scale and mature engineering systems
- better long-term credibility with later `Director`, `VP`, `SVP`, and `BU CTO` searches

Important bias:

`Engineering Manager` at a top-tier company is usually a better long-term CTO bridge than `Principal` at the same company, because it keeps the management and org-building story alive.

### Lane 3: Prestige IC Lane

Useful for comp and brand, but not the primary route to large-company CTO.

Target titles:

- `Principal Engineer`
- `Senior Staff Engineer`
- `Distinguished Engineer`
- `Chief Architect`

Take this lane selectively when:

- the brand is truly elite
- the compensation is clearly outstanding
- the technical scope is large and visible
- the role is strong enough to justify a temporary pause in the executive ladder

Risk:

This lane builds technical-authority proof more than executive-operating proof.

## Priority Order

The three lanes are active, but they are not equal.

1. Best case: a role that combines `Lane 1` and `Lane 2`
2. Next best: strong `Lane 1` executive bridge role
3. Next best: strong `Lane 2` management role at a top-tier brand
4. Selective upside: `Lane 3` prestige IC role

Practical interpretation:

- a `Director` / `Senior Director` / `Head of Engineering` role at a top-tier company is the ideal overlap target
- a great `Engineering Manager` role at Netflix-scale companies is still a legitimate scale-up move
- a `Principal` / `Distinguished` role should be pursued when the company and package are strong enough to justify the detour

Suggested search weighting:

- `Lane 1`: `55%`
- `Lane 2`: `30%`
- `Lane 3`: `15%`

Sorting rule:

Any role that clearly satisfies both `Lane 1` and `Lane 2` gets bumped to the top of the review queue.

## 2-5 Year Ladder

### Primary Executive Ladder

`0-12 months`

- land `VP Engineering`, `Head of Engineering`, `Senior Director`, `Director`, or mid-market `CTO` with materially more scale than current and prior startups

`12-30 months`

- move into `SVP Engineering`, larger-scope `VP Engineering`, `Chief Engineering Officer`, or `Business Unit CTO`

`30-60 months`

- pursue `CTO`, `EVP Technology`, `EVP Engineering`, or equivalent enterprise technology leadership role

### Brand Management Detour

`0-24 months`

- land `Engineering Manager`, `Senior Engineering Manager`, `Director`, or platform/reliability/developer-productivity leadership role at a top-tier engineering brand

`18-36 months`

- convert that brand-and-scale proof into `Director`, `Senior Director`, `Head of Engineering`, or `VP Engineering` at a serious growth, enterprise, or platform-heavy company

`36-60 months`

- pursue `VP`, `SVP`, `BU CTO`, then larger-company `CTO` / `EVP`

### Explicit Tradeoff

The brand-management detour is slower on title but faster on scale credibility.

## Role Screening Rules

Prefer roles that offer at least two of these:

- customer-facing application ownership
- greenfield product building or major application modernization scope
- strong partnership with Product / Design / GTM on software delivery
- bigger org size
- more leadership layers
- clearer budget ownership
- stronger compliance / governance responsibility
- stronger executive-team interaction
- more visible business-critical systems
- stronger external brand signal

Be skeptical when a role has any of these:

- same scope as prior startup CTO roles
- title looks senior but team is too small
- pure internal-tools / corporate-IT scope without exceptional brand, pay, or trajectory upside
- security / identity scope that is far from product or application delivery and does not clearly improve the long-term path
- no management responsibility and no truly elite brand value
- unclear budget or decision rights
- comp clearly below floor without a compelling brand or trajectory reason

## Compensation Guardrails

Default cash target:

- ideal full-time target: `290k+` base or guaranteed cash
- practical W-2 floor: `265k-270k` base when benefits are strong

Brand-exception rule:

- it is acceptable to stretch below the ideal target only when the brand, scope, and future leverage are unusually strong
- do not casually accept a comp haircut that does not buy real scale or brand value

## Positioning Guidance

Default external story:

`Hands-on CTO / engineering executive who builds greenfield and modernization-heavy applications, scales product and application teams, improves delivery systems, and turns workflow-heavy business problems into durable software products.`

For executive roles, emphasize:

- product / application engineering leadership
- greenfield builds and major application modernization
- customer-facing software delivery
- Product / Design / Engineering operating cadence
- org design
- hiring and coaching
- delivery systems
- budget and cost governance
- security / compliance / governance
- cross-functional executive alignment

For brand-management roles, emphasize:

- product/application team leadership when available
- people leadership
- platform and reliability ownership
- strong engineering systems
- developer productivity
- operating rigor at scale

For prestige IC roles, emphasize:

- application architecture and product-platform depth
- distributed systems depth
- AI/tooling/platform architecture
- modernization and technical authority

## Working Rule For The Search

Do not think in terms of title purity.

Think in terms of what proof the next role gives for the role after that.

The ideal target is a role that:

- moves toward large-company `CTO` / `EVP Technology`
- improves scale credibility
- preferably adds top-tier brand value
- maintains or improves compensation
